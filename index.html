Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)
Invest a fixed amount every month into a mutual fund. Your money grows through rupee cost averaging — you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when high. Ideal for salaried individuals building long-term wealth with consistent monthly discipline, no matter the market condition.

Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)
Already have a corpus? SWP lets you withdraw a fixed amount every month while the remaining balance continues to earn returns. Think of it like a self-made pension — perfect for retirees or anyone wanting regular income from investments without liquidating everything at once.

Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal (IDCW)
Previously known as the "Dividend" option. The fund periodically distributes a portion of its profits back to you as income. Unlike SWP, the payout is not fixed — it depends on the fund's declared distribution rate and NAV. Suitable for investors wanting occasional passive income while staying invested.

NCD + SIP Strategy
Invest a lumpsum in an NCD (Non-Convertible Debenture) which pays fixed interest. Instead of spending that interest, you reinvest it as SIP into a Mutual Fund. At maturity you get your NCD principal back plus a mutual fund corpus built from reinvested interest — capital protection with wealth creation.
