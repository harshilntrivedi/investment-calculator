Tax Guide for Investors
Understand how your investments are taxed, compare old vs new tax regime, estimate your LTCG tax, calculate ELSS tax savings, and plan your SIP redemption smartly. All in simple language — no jargon.
ⓘ Mutual fund taxation depends on type of fund (Equity or Debt) and how long you hold it. Here's everything you need to know in one place.
Equity Mutual Funds (65%+ in stocks)
Holding PeriodTypeTax RateExample
Less than 1 yearShort Term (STCG)20% on gainsBought Jan, sold Oct — pay 20% on profit
More than 1 yearLong Term (LTCG)12.5% on gains above ₹1.25 LakhFirst ₹1.25L gain is tax-free every year
Debt Mutual Funds (bonds, FDs etc.)
Holding PeriodTypeTax RateNote
Any durationAlways STCGAdded to your income slabIf you're in 30% bracket, you pay 30% tax
Hybrid Funds
Fund TypeEquity AllocationTaxed As
Aggressive Hybrid65%+ equityEquity fund rules apply
Conservative HybridLess than 65% equityDebt fund rules apply
Balanced Advantage FundVaries (dynamic)Usually Equity fund rules
💡 Key Tip: IDCW (Dividend) payouts are added to your income and taxed at your slab rate. Growth option is generally more tax-efficient for long-term investors.
ⓘ India offers two tax regimes. You can choose the one that saves you more tax — but you can only switch once per year. Here's a simple comparison to help you decide.

With deductions and exemptions

❌ More paperwork and proofs

❌ No HRA, 80C, 80D benefits

💡 Remember: With the new regime, income up to ₹12 Lakh is effectively zero tax thanks to Section 87A rebate. Above ₹12L, normal slab rates apply.
ⓘ LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains) applies when you sell equity mutual fund units held for more than 1 year. You get ₹1.25 Lakh exemption per year — only gains above this are taxed at 12.5%.
